    All Information About Dam

    Type of Dam: Gravity Dam: The Tungabhadra Dam is a gravity dam, meaning it relies on its weight to resist the force of the water. Height: 49.38 meters (162 feet): The height of the dam from the foundation.
    Length: 2,441 meters (8,010 feet): The total length of the dam.
    Reservoir Capacity: Full Reservoir Level (FRL): 497.74 meters (1,633 feet). Gross Storage Capacity: Approximately 3,760 million cubic meters (MCM).
    Spillway Capacity: 11,500 cubic meters per second (cumecs): The maximum discharge capacity of the spillway. Functional Characteristics
    Purpose:Irrigation: Provides water for irrigation to the agricultural lands in the Tungabhadra Command Area. Hydropower Generation: Generates hydroelectric power. Flood Control: Helps in controlling floods during the monsoon season. Water Supply: Supplies water for domestic and industrial use.
    Power Generation: Installed Capacity: The dam has several hydroelectric power stations with a combined capacity of around 72 MW. Geographic and Environmental Characteristics
    Location: River: Built across the Tungabhadra River, a tributary of the Krishna River. District: Situated in the Ballari District of Karnataka and Kurnool District of Andhra Pradesh. Coordinates: Approximately 15° 15' N latitude and 76° 20' E longitude. Catchment Area: 28,177 square kilometers (10,882 square miles): The area from which the dam collects runoff water.
